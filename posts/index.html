Understanding Perspective-N-Points

Introduction The Perspective-n-Point (PnP) problem is the problem of estimating the relative pose between an object and the camera, given a set of correspondences between 3D points and their projections on the image plane. It is a fundamental problem that was first studied in the photogrammetry literature, and later on studied in the context of computer vision. In this post, I will present a few solvers (among many), discuss their proofs and also show some concise implementations.
